## Retention Philosophy & Core Principles

### Our Core Belief
Every customer represents a long-term relationship, not a single transaction. Retaining an existing customer costs 5-7 times less than acquiring a new one. Our goal is to make customers feel valued, heard, and protected throughout their entire journey with Elite Insurance Partners.

### The Five Pillars of Retention
1. **Proactive Communication** - Reach customers before they reach for the door
2. **Personalized Value** - Show individual customers their unique benefits
3. **Friction Reduction** - Make staying easier than leaving
4. **Trust Building** - Demonstrate reliability in every interaction
5. **Continuous Improvement** - Learn from every departure and near-miss

### Key Retention Mindset
- **Listen First**: Understand the real reason behind dissatisfaction
- **Empathize Always**: Acknowledge frustration before problem-solving
- **Act Quickly**: Speed matters when customers are considering leaving
- **Follow Through**: Promises made must be promises kept
- **Learn Constantly**: Every saved or lost customer teaches us something

## Renewal Process & Timing Strategies

### 90-Day Renewal Strategy

**60 Days Before Renewal (Early Bird Phase)**

**Purpose**: Plant seeds of loyalty and address concerns early

**Communication Strategy**:
```
"Hi [Name], I'm calling from Elite Insurance Partners. I wanted to reach
out personally because your policy renewal is coming up in about 60 days.
I know renewal time can sometimes feel overwhelming with all the quotes
and options out there, so I wanted to get ahead of it and make sure
you're completely satisfied with your coverage.

Can I ask you a few quick questions about how everything's been going
with us this year?"

**Key Questions**:
- "Have you had any claims experience with us? How did we handle it?"
- "Has anything changed in your life - new car, moved, got married?"
- "Are you aware of all the discounts you're currently receiving?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to renew with us?"
```

**Action Items**:
- Score customer satisfaction (1-10 scale)
- Document any concerns for immediate follow-up
- Check for discount optimization opportunities
- Update customer preferences and contact information
- Schedule follow-up call if needed

**Purpose**: Present renewal terms and demonstrate value

**Communication Strategy**:
```

## At-Risk Customer Identification

### Early Warning Signals

**Behavioral Red Flags**:
- Decreased engagement with communications (unopened emails, missed calls)
- Multiple quote requests on comparison websites (detected through integrations)
- Questions about policy cancellation procedures
- Requests for insurance ID cards shortly before renewal
- Sudden interest in coverage details after long period of inactivity
- Short, dismissive responses to outreach attempts
- Changes in payment method or payment delays
- Removal of automatic payment enrollment

**Stated Concerns** (Document immediately):
- Mentions of rate shopping or receiving other quotes
- Complaints about premium increases
- Dissatisfaction with claims handling
- References to financial hardship
- Life changes (moving out of state, selling vehicle, etc.)
- Competitor comparisons or name-dropping
- Requests to reduce coverage to minimum limits

**Data-Driven Risk Indicators**:
- Customer satisfaction score below 7/10
- No policy interactions in last 90 days
- Recent claim with negative feedback
- Rate increase of 15% or more
- Multiple customer service escalations
- Competitor policy detected (through data partnerships)
- Social media negative mentions
- Payment autopay cancellation

### Risk Scoring System

**Low Risk (Score 1-3)**: Standard retention process
- Engaged customer with recent positive interactions
- Satisfaction score 8-10
- Utilizes digital tools regularly
- Multiple policies or referrals provided

**Medium Risk (Score 4-6)**: Enhanced retention required
- Mixed engagement signals
- Satisfaction score 6-7
- Some negative feedback but nothing severe
- Minimal policy interactions

**High Risk (Score 7-10)**: Intensive retention intervention
- Multiple red flags present
- Satisfaction score below 6
- Explicit mention of leaving or shopping
- Recent negative experience unresolved

### At-Risk Customer Intervention Protocol

**Step 1: Rapid Response (Within 24 hours)**
Assign dedicated retention specialist for high-risk customers

**Step 2: Diagnostic Conversation**
```
"Hi [Name], I noticed [specific trigger - payment change, service
request, feedback, etc.], and I wanted to reach out personally to make
sure everything is okay with your insurance experience.

I'm not calling to sell you anything - I genuinely want to understand
if there's something we could be doing better for you.

Can you share with me what's on your mind about your coverage with us?"
```

**Step 3: Active Listening & Documentation**
- Let customer speak without interruption
- Acknowledge all concerns without defensiveness
- Document every specific complaint or need
- Confirm understanding: "What I'm hearing is... Is that accurate?"

**Step 4: Immediate Action Plan**
```
"Thank you for being honest with me, [Name]. Here's what I'm going to
do immediately:

1. [Specific action item with timeline]
2. [Specific action item with timeline]
3. [Specific action item with timeline]

I'm going to personally oversee this and follow up with you by [date].
Can I have your permission to make these changes and check back with you?"
```

**Step 5: Follow-Through & Verification**
- Execute promised actions ahead of deadline
- Document all changes in customer file
- Schedule follow-up call to confirm satisfaction
- Continue monitoring for 90 days post-intervention

## Cancellation Request Handling

### The Golden Rule of Save Attempts
Never make the customer feel trapped or pressured. Our goal is to understand, address, and offer alternatives - but ultimately respect their decision.

### Cancellation Call Flow

**Step 1: Acknowledge & Empathize**
```
"I understand you're calling to cancel your policy. Before we process
that, I'm required to ask a few questions, but I want you to know that
I respect your decision. My job is just to make sure we haven't missed
anything that might change your mind. Is it okay if I ask you some
questions first?"
```

**Step 2: Understand the Real Reason**

**Critical Discovery Questions**:
```
1. "Can you help me understand what's prompting this decision?"

2. "When did you first start thinking about canceling?"

3. "Have you already found alternative coverage?"
   - If yes: "Can you tell me about what they're offering?"
   - If no: "Are you planning to go without insurance, or still shopping?"

4. "Is this primarily about price, coverage, service, or something else?"

5. "Was there a specific experience or interaction that led to this?"

6. "On a scale of 1-10, how firm is your decision? Is there anything
   that might make you reconsider?"
```

### When to Accept the Cancellation

**Signs You Should Let Go**:
- Customer has already purchased alternative coverage
- Customer is moving outside your licensed states
- Customer is hostile or verbally abusive despite your attempts
- Customer has made firm decision after multiple reasonable offers
- Legal or regulatory requirements prevent you from continuing coverage

**Graceful Exit Script**:
```
"[Name], I respect your decision. While I'm disappointed to see you go,
I want to make this process as easy as possible for you.

A few important things:

1. Your cancellation will be effective [date]. You'll receive a refund
   of [amount] within [timeframe].

2. Make sure your new coverage is active before this cancellation date
   to avoid any gaps.

3. I'll email you a complete summary of your policy history and
   claims-free record - keep this for your records as it can help with
   future insurers.

4. If anything changes in the future, I'd welcome the opportunity to
   earn back your business. You can always call me directly at [number].

Is there anything else I can help you with before we finalize this?"
```

**Post-Cancellation Follow-Up** (30 days later):
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Agent Name] from Elite Insurance Partners. I wanted
to reach out and see how things are going with your new insurance
arrangement.

I'm not calling to pressure you - I genuinely hope you're happy with
your decision. But if anything hasn't gone as expected, or if you'd
like to explore coming back, I'm here and would love to talk.

Also, if there's any feedback about why you left that you didn't feel
comfortable sharing before, I'd really appreciate hearing it now. It
helps us improve for our other customers."
```

## Rate Increase Communication

### The Rate Increase Challenge
Rate increases are the number one reason customers shop for new insurance. How we communicate these increases determines whether customers feel respected or gouged.

### Proactive Rate Increase Strategy

**30-45 Days Before Renewal (Before Official Notice)**

**Script**:
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Agent Name] from Elite Insurance Partners. I'm
calling with something I wish I had better news about, but I want to
be transparent with you before you receive your official renewal notice.

Your premium is going up by [amount/percentage] at your next renewal.
I know that's frustrating, and I want to explain why this happened and
what we can do about it.

Do you have a few minutes to go through this together?"
```

### Explanation Frameworks by Reason

---

**Scenario 1: Claims History**

**Script**:
```
"The reason for your increase is related to the [type] claim you filed
on [date]. I know this feels unfair because that's exactly why you have
insurance, but here's what's happening:

When we pay out a claim, it signals to our actuaries that there's a
higher risk of future claims. The increase of [amount] is essentially
the statistical cost of that elevated risk over the next policy term.

Now, here's the important part: This increase is temporary. As that
