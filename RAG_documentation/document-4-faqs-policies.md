# Claims Process Overview

## How to Report a Claim

### Immediate Steps After an Accident

**Step 1: Ensure Safety**
- Check for injuries and call 911 if anyone is hurt
- Move to a safe location if possible
- Turn on hazard lights
- Set up warning triangles or flares if available

**Step 2: Document the Scene**
- Take photos of all vehicles involved (damage from multiple angles)
- Photograph license plates, VINs, and insurance cards
- Capture the accident scene (street signs, traffic signals, skid marks)
- Get witness names and contact information
- Note weather conditions, time of day, and traffic conditions

**Step 3: Exchange Information**
- Name, phone number, and address of all drivers
- Insurance company and policy numbers
- Vehicle make, model, year, and license plate
- Driver's license numbers
- DO NOT admit fault or discuss details with other parties
- Be polite but limit conversation to factual information exchange

**Step 4: Report to Police (If Required)**
- File police report if required by state law or local ordinance
- Typically required for accidents involving:
  - Injuries or death
  - Property damage over $1,000-$2,500 (varies by state)
  - Hit and run
  - Suspected DUI
  - Government vehicles
- Get police report number and officer's information

**Step 5: Report to Elite Insurance Partners**

Call our 24/7 claims hotline immediately:
- **Phone:** 1-800-EIP-CLAIM (1-800-347-2524)
- **Mobile App:** Tap "File a Claim" button
- **Online Portal:** Login and select "Report New Claim"

You'll need:
- Policy number
- Date, time, and location of accident
- Description of what happened
- Contact information for all parties involved
- Police report number (if applicable)
- Photos/documentation collected

## Claims Process Timeline

### Phase 1: Initial Report and Assignment (0-2 Hours)

**What Happens:**
- Claim is logged in our system with unique claim number
- Initial review by claims intake specialist
- Dedicated adjuster assigned based on claim type and complexity
- Automated confirmation sent via email and text

**What You'll Receive:**
- Claim number (reference for all future communications)
- Adjuster name and direct contact information
- Initial instructions and next steps
- Rental car authorization (if applicable to coverage)

**Timeline:** Within 2 hours of reporting claim, 24/7

---

### Phase 2: Initial Contact and Assessment (2-24 Hours)

**What Happens:**
- Your assigned adjuster will contact you directly
- Review of coverage applicable to the claim
- Discussion of the accident details
- Explanation of the claims process and timeline
- Scheduling of vehicle inspection (if needed)
- Verification of any injuries requiring medical attention

**What You'll Be Asked:**
- Detailed description of the accident
- Whether vehicle is drivable
- Whether you need rental car
- Preferred repair shop (or use of certified network)
- Any injuries sustained

**What You Should Do:**
- Answer all questions honestly and completely
- Provide any additional photos or documentation
- Ask questions about anything unclear
- Confirm preferred method of contact
- Discuss rental car needs

**Timeline:** Initial contact within 2-4 hours, full assessment within 24 hours

---

### Phase 3: Vehicle Inspection and Damage Assessment (1-3 Days)

**What Happens:**
- Vehicle inspection scheduled at convenient location
- Physical assessment by appraiser or adjuster
- Damage documentation and photography
- Preliminary repair estimate generated
- Total loss determination (if applicable)

**Inspection Options:**
- **Mobile Inspection:** Adjuster comes to your location
- **Drive-In Inspection:** Visit certified inspection center
- **Shop Inspection:** At your chosen repair facility
- **Photo Inspection:** Submit detailed photos through app (minor claims)

**Total Loss Criteria:**
A vehicle is typically considered a total loss when:
- Repair costs exceed 70-75% of vehicle's actual cash value (varies by state)
- Vehicle is unsafe to repair
- Frame or structural damage is severe
- Airbags deployed and repair costs are high

**Timeline:**
- Inspection scheduled: 1-2 days
- Initial estimate completed: 24-48 hours after inspection

---

### Phase 4: Repair Authorization or Settlement (3-5 Days)

**For Repairable Vehicles:**

**What Happens:**
- Final repair estimate approved
- Rental car authorization extended for duration of repairs
- Repair shop selected (your choice or certified network)
- Repairs authorized and scheduled
- Supplemental damage assessment during teardown (if needed)

**Your Repair Options:**
1. **Certified Network Shop:**
   - Pre-negotiated rates (may reduce your out-of-pocket)
   - Guaranteed workmanship (we stand behind repairs)
   - Streamlined process and direct billing
   - Priority scheduling

2. **Your Preferred Shop:**
   - You have the right to choose any licensed repair facility
   - We'll work directly with them on estimate
   - Same quality of service and attention
   - May require you to manage some coordination

**What You Pay:**
- Your deductible (paid directly to repair shop upon completion)
- Any coverage limits excess (if applicable)

**For Total Loss Vehicles:**

**What Happens:**
- Actual cash value (ACV) calculated based on:
  - Pre-accident condition
  - Mileage and age
  - Local market values for comparable vehicles
  - Recent sale prices of similar vehicles
- Settlement offer presented with detailed valuation report
- Negotiation period (if you disagree with valuation)
- Lienholder payoff (if financed/leased)
- Settlement check issued

**Settlement Timeline:**
- Initial offer: 3-5 business days after total loss determination
- Negotiation period: 5-10 business days
- Final settlement: 24-48 hours after acceptance

**What You Receive:**
- Actual cash value minus:
  - Your deductible
  - Outstanding loan balance (if applicable)
  - Prior damage deductions (if any)
- Sales tax assistance (varies by state)
- Title transfer assistance

**Timeline:**
- Repair authorization: 3-5 days
- Total loss settlement offer: 3-5 days

---

### Phase 5: Repairs and Quality Assurance (7-21 Days)

**During Repairs:**
- Regular updates from repair shop and adjuster
- Supplemental damage review (common during teardown)
- Additional authorization if needed
- Estimated completion date provided
- Rental car coverage continues

**Supplemental Damage:**
- Hidden damage discovered during disassembly
- Requires additional estimate and approval
- Does NOT restart your deductible
- May extend repair timeline

**Quality Assurance Process:**
- Final inspection by adjuster (if certified network)
- Repair quality verification
- Computer diagnostics and test drive
- Warranty documentation

**What You Should Do:**
- Stay in contact with repair shop
- Report any concerns immediately
- Review completed repairs before accepting vehicle
- Test all functions (lights, locks, windows, etc.)
- Keep all repair documentation

**Timeline:** 7-21 days depending on damage severity and parts availability

---

### Phase 6: Vehicle Return and Claim Closure (21+ Days)

**Final Steps:**
- Pick up repaired vehicle from shop
- Pay deductible directly to repair shop
- Return rental car (same day as pickup)
- Sign off on completed repairs
- Receive repair warranty information
- Claim closed in our system

**Post-Repair Support:**
- 30-day follow-up call from adjuster
- Repair warranty (typically 12 months or 12,000 miles through certified network)
- Reopen claim if issues arise within warranty period
- Customer satisfaction survey

**If Settlement Was Issued:**
- Submit title and vehicle for salvage
- Confirm lienholder received payoff
- Cancel or adjust coverage on totaled vehicle
- Update policy to reflect vehicle removal

**Timeline:** Claim typically closes within 30 days of vehicle return or settlement acceptance

## Claim Types and Specific Processes

### Collision Claims (At-Fault Accidents)

**Coverage Used:** Collision Coverage

**Process:**
- Report accident immediately
- Provide statement of facts
- Your deductible applies
- No determination of fault needed for your vehicle repairs
- Rates may increase at renewal (unless accident forgiveness applies)

**Deductible Options:**
- Paid directly to repair shop when picking up vehicle
- Deducted from settlement if total loss
- Only one deductible per accident (not per vehicle if multiple vehicles on policy)

**Timeline:** Standard process (7-21 days for repairs)

---

### Collision Claims (Not At-Fault Accidents)

**Coverage Used:** Collision Coverage (initially)

**Process Option 1 - File with Your Insurance:**
- Faster process
- You pay your deductible upfront
- We pursue reimbursement from at-fault party's insurance (subrogation)
- Deductible refunded when we recover funds (typically 3-6 months)
- No rate increase since not at fault

**Process Option 2 - File with At-Fault Party's Insurance:**
- No deductible required
- May take longer to process
- You manage communication with other insurance company
- No impact to your rates
- May face delays if fault is disputed

**Our Recommendation:** File with us for faster service, we'll handle recovery

**Subrogation Process:**
- We pay your claim minus deductible
- Our subrogation team pursues at-fault party's insurer
- When recovered, we refund your deductible
- We notify you of recovery status monthly

**Timeline:**
- Repairs: Standard 7-21 days
- Deductible recovery: 3-6 months average

---

### Comprehensive Claims (Non-Collision Damage)

**Coverage Used:** Comprehensive Coverage

**Common Comprehensive Claims:**
- Theft or vandalism
- Weather damage (hail, flood, tornado)
- Fire or explosion
- Hitting an animal (deer, etc.)
- Falling objects (tree, debris)
- Glass damage (windshield, windows)

**Process:**
- Report claim immediately
- File police report (required for theft/vandalism)
- Provide documentation of damage
- Your deductible applies (except glass in some states)
- Typically does NOT affect rates

**Theft Claims - Special Process:**

**Immediate Steps:**
1. Report to police within 24 hours
2. Report to Elite Insurance within 24 hours
3. Provide keys, title, and registration information
4. Keep police report number

**Waiting Period:**
- Most states require 15-30 day waiting period before settlement
- Allows time for vehicle recovery
- Rental car coverage provided during waiting period (subject to policy limits)
- If recovered, we repair damage; if not, we settle total loss

**Recovered Vehicle Options:**
- If found before settlement: We repair and return to you
- If found after settlement: Vehicle belongs to insurance company
- You may purchase recovered vehicle back if desired

**Timeline:**
- Glass claims: 1-3 days
- Minor comprehensive: 5-10 days
- Theft claims: 15-30 day waiting period plus settlement time

---

### Glass Claims (Windshield/Window Damage)

**Coverage Used:** Comprehensive Coverage

**Special Glass Provisions:**
- Some policies have $0 deductible for glass
- Some states require $0 glass deductible by law
- Repair vs. replacement determination:
  - Chips smaller than quarter: Usually repairable
  - Cracks longer than 6 inches: Usually require replacement
  - Damage in driver's line of sight: Usually require replacement

**Process:**
- Call glass claims line: 1-800-EIP-GLASS
- Schedule appointment with network glass shop
- Mobile service available (they come to you)
- Typically same-day or next-day service
- Direct billing to insurance (you pay deductible only)

**Repair vs. Replace:**
- Repair: 30-45 minutes, $0-50 typical cost
- Replacement: 2-4 hours, deductible applies unless $0 glass coverage

**Timeline:** Same day or next day service

---

### Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Claims

**When Used:**
- At-fault driver has no insurance
- At-fault driver's insurance is insufficient for damages
- Hit and run accidents (driver unknown)

**Coverage Details:**
- Covers bodily injury to you and passengers
- Covers property damage (in states where included)
- Subject to your policy limits
- May have separate deductible

**Process:**
1. File police report (required)
2. Report to our claims department
3. Provide all information about at-fault party (if known)
4. Medical documentation (if injuries)
5. We investigate and determine coverage
6. Settlement based on injuries/damages and policy limits

**Special Requirements:**
- Police report mandatory
- Must exhaust at-fault party's coverage first (if any)
- Proof of other party's insurance status
- Medical records and bills
- Lost wage documentation

**Timeline:** 30-90 days depending on injury extent and documentation

---

**What's Covered:**

**What's NOT Covered:**

**Documentation Needed:**

**Payment Options:**

### Rental Car Coverage Claims

**Coverage Used:** Rental Car Reimbursement

**When Coverage Applies:**
- Covered loss (collision or comprehensive)
- Vehicle is not drivable
- Vehicle is in shop for covered repairs
- Total loss during settlement period

**Coverage Limits:**
- Typical: $30-50 per day
- Maximum: 30-45 days
- Actual rental cost up to daily limit

**Process:**
1. Claim adjuster authorizes rental
2. You receive rental authorization email/letter
3. Pick up rental from preferred provider (Enterprise, Hertz, etc.)
4. Present authorization and driver's license

**Rental Class:**
- Economy or compact car (similar to your vehicle size)
- Upgrades at your expense
- One rental vehicle per claim

**When to Return Rental:**
- Same day as vehicle repair completion
- Within 24 hours of total loss settlement offer
- When coverage limits exhausted
- Late fees are your responsibility

**Timeline:** Rental available immediately upon authorization

## Claims Denial Reasons and Appeals

### Common Reasons for Claim Denial

**1. Coverage Exclusions**
- Damage not covered by policy type
- Intentional damage
- Wear and tear or mechanical breakdown
- Commercial use without commercial policy
- Racing or speed contests

**2. Policy Lapses**
- Accident occurred during coverage gap
- Non-payment of premium
- Policy canceled prior to loss date

**3. Excluded Driver**
- Driver not listed on policy and explicitly excluded
- Driver without valid license
- Unlisted household member

**4. Fraud or Misrepresentation**
- False statements on claim
- Staged accident
- Exaggerated damages
- False theft report

**5. Late Reporting**
- Claim reported beyond policy timeframe
- Insufficient cooperation with investigation

**6. Outside Policy Period**
- Loss occurred before policy start date
- Loss occurred after policy end date

---

### Claims Appeal Process

**Step 1: Review Denial Letter (Day 1)**
- Carefully read reason for denial
- Review policy language cited
- Gather supporting documentation
- Note appeal deadline (typically 30-60 days)

**Step 2: Contact Claims Adjuster (Days 1-3)**
- Call adjuster to discuss denial
- Ask questions about decision
- Present any new information
- Request reconsideration

**Step 3: Submit Formal Appeal (Days 7-14)**
- Write formal appeal letter including:
  - Policy number and claim number
  - Summary of accident/loss
  - Reason you believe claim should be covered
  - Supporting documentation
  - Policy language supporting your position
- Send via certified mail or secure upload

**Required Documentation:**
- Copy of denial letter
- Policy declarations page
- Photos and evidence
- Police reports
- Estimates or repair bills
- Witness statements
- Any new information not previously provided

**Step 4: Claims Management Review (Days 14-30)**
- Senior claims manager reviews appeal
- Independent evaluation of coverage
- Review of all documentation
- Additional investigation if needed
- Written decision issued

**Step 5: Executive Escalation (Days 30-45)**
If second denial:
- Request executive review
- Claims director evaluates
- Final internal decision

**Step 6: External Options (Days 45+)**
If still denied:
- State insurance department complaint
- Independent appraisal (if dispute is about damage amount)
- Mediation
- Arbitration (if in policy)
- Legal action

**Contact for Appeals:**
- Appeals Department: 1-800-EIP-APPEAL
- Email: appeals@eliteinsurance.com
- Mail: Elite Insurance Partners, Appeals Department, PO Box 12345, City, ST 12345

---

### Dispute Resolution for Damage Amount

**If You Disagree with Repair Estimate:**

**Step 1: Request Re-Inspection**
- Contact adjuster with specific concerns
- Point out any missed damage
- Provide additional photos
- Request second inspection

**Step 2: Get Independent Estimate**
- Take vehicle to trusted repair shop
- Get written estimate
- Submit to adjuster for review
- Insurance will evaluate differences

**Step 3: Appraisal Process**
- If agreement can't be reached
- Both parties hire independent appraiser
- Appraisers hire neutral umpire
- Three-party panel determines value
- Decision is binding
- Costs split between parties

**For Total Loss Value Disputes:**

**Step 1: Review Valuation Report**
- Check comparable vehicles used
- Verify accuracy of your vehicle's condition
- Note mileage, options, and features
- Review market analysis

**Step 2: Provide Counter-Evidence**
- Find comparable vehicles for sale
- Show recent sales data
- Document special features or upgrades
- Submit pre-accident appraisal (if available)

**Step 3: Negotiate**
- Present evidence to adjuster
- Justify higher value
- Be prepared to compromise
- Focus on legitimate market value differences

**Step 4: Request Appraisal**
- If negotiation fails
- Same process as damage amount dispute
- Appraisers determine actual cash value
- Decision is binding

## Special Claim Situations

### Multi-Vehicle Accidents

**Your Policy Coverage:**
- Your collision coverage pays for your vehicle (minus deductible)
- Liability coverage pays for other vehicles if you're at fault
- Each vehicle subject to one deductible

**Liability Determination:**
- May be shared fault (percentage assigned)
- Comparative negligence in many states
- Your coverage applies to your percentage of fault
- Subrogation between insurers for property damage

**Process:**
- Report all vehicles involved
- Separate claims for each vehicle
- One adjuster coordinates both claims
- May have different timelines

---

### Accidents Out of State

**Coverage:**
- Your policy follows you anywhere in US and Canada
- Coverage limits apply regardless of location
- Rental coverage extends out of state

**Special Considerations:**
- State minimum requirements vary
- File police report per local laws
- Different repair shops and networks
- May require local adjusters

**Process:**
1. Follow local accident reporting laws
2. Report to Elite Insurance immediately
3. We'll assign local or traveling adjuster
4. Rental car arranged locally if needed
5. Repairs can be done locally or at home (your choice)

---

### Accidents While Towing

**Coverage:**
- Collision: Covers damage to your vehicle and attached trailer
- Liability: Covers damage you cause to others while towing
- Requirements: Trailer must be properly attached and under weight limits

**Special Considerations:**
- Borrowed trailer: May be covered
- Commercial towing: May need commercial policy
- Overweight loads: May void coverage

**Documentation Needed:**
- Trailer ownership and registration
- Towing capacity verification
- Weight of load
- Proper hitch and connection proof

---

### Accidents in Rental Cars

**Coverage Options:**
1. **Your Personal Policy Extends to Rental:**
   - Collision and comprehensive transfer
   - Liability coverage applies
   - Same deductibles
   - Medical payments coverage applies

2. **Rental Company Insurance:**
   - Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) from rental company
   - Liability Supplement Insurance (LSI)
   - Personal Effects Coverage

**Our Recommendation:**
- Decline rental company insurance (you're covered)
- Exception: Luxury or exotic rentals (confirm coverage first)

**Process:**
1. Report accident to rental company and police
2. Report to Elite Insurance
3. We handle claim with rental company
4. Your deductible applies
5. Loss of use charges covered

---

### Accidents with Company Vehicles

**Personal Vehicle Used for Work:**
- Personal policy covers commuting
- Business use: Need business use declaration
- Commercial activities: Need commercial policy

**Company-Owned Vehicle:**
- Company's commercial policy is primary
- Your personal policy may provide excess coverage
- Report to company's insurer first

**Process:**
- Clarify vehicle ownership
- Determine which policy applies
- Both insurers may coordinate
- Report to both if unclear

# General FAQs

## Coverage Questions

### Q: What does "full coverage" mean?

**A:** "Full coverage" is not an industry term, but typically refers to:
- **Liability Coverage:** Covers damage you cause to others
- **Comprehensive Coverage:** Covers non-collision damage to your vehicle (theft, weather, vandalism)
- **Collision Coverage:** Covers collision damage to your vehicle

This combination protects you for most scenarios. However, "full coverage" doesn't mean everything is covered - there are still exclusions and limits.

**What's NOT included in "full coverage":**
- Mechanical breakdowns
- Normal wear and tear
- Intentional damage
- Racing or commercial use
- Custom equipment (unless specifically added)

---

### Q: Do I really need more than state minimum insurance?

**A:** State minimums are often inadequate. Here's why:

**State Minimum Problems:**
- Minimums set decades ago, haven't kept up with costs
- Average serious accident costs $50,000-$200,000+
- You're personally liable for amounts exceeding your coverage
- They can garnish wages, seize assets, put liens on your home

**Example:**
- State minimum: $25,000 per person
- Serious accident: Other driver has $75,000 in medical bills
- You're personally liable for: $50,000

**Our Recommendation:**
- Minimum: 100/300/100
- Better: 250/500/250
- Consider umbrella policy for additional protection

---

### Q: What's the difference between comprehensive and collision?

**A:** Both cover damage to your vehicle, but for different reasons:

### Q: What is "actual cash value" and how is it calculated?

**A:** Actual Cash Value (ACV) is what your car is worth today:

**Formula:**
ACV = Replacement Cost - Depreciation

**Factors:**

- Mileage
- Condition before accident
- Local market values
- Recent sales of comparable vehicles
- Optional equipment and features
- Prior damage

**Not Included:**
- What you paid originally
- What you owe on loan
- Emotional value
- Your replacement cost

**Example:**
- 2020 Honda Accord, 35,000 miles
- Excellent condition
- Purchased new for $28,000
- Current ACV: $22,000 (depreciation)
- Your loan balance: $24,000
- Insurance pays: $22,000
- You owe: $2,000 (why gap insurance helps)

**Depreciation Factors:**
- Age: ~20% first year, 10-15% per year after
- Mileage: Below/above average affects value
- Condition: Excellent vs. poor makes difference
- Market: Supply and demand in your area
- Modifications: May increase or decrease value

**Valuation Tools Used:**
- CCC ONE
- Mitchell
- NADA
- Kelly Blue Book
- Local market analysis

### Q: What do the liability numbers mean (e.g., 100/300/100)?

## Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage

### Q: What's the difference between uninsured and underinsured motorist coverage?

**A:** Both protect you from drivers with inadequate insurance:

**Uninsured Motorist (UM):**
- Other driver has no insurance
- Hit-and-run accidents (driver unknown)
- Other driver's insurance company insolvent

**Underinsured Motorist (UIM):**
- Other driver has insurance, but not enough
- Their limits too low for your injuries/damages
- Your UIM pays the difference (up to your limits)

**Example - Underinsured:**
- You're hit by at-fault driver
- Your injuries: $150,000
- Their insurance limit: $50,000 (state minimum)
- Their insurance pays: $50,000
- **Your UIM pays:** $100,000 (you have 250/500 UIM)
- Total recovered: $150,000

**Example - Uninsured:**
- Hit by driver with no insurance
- Your injuries: $100,000
- Their insurance pays: $0
- **Your UM pays:** $100,000
- Total recovered: $100,000

**Coverage Options:**
- Usually sold together (UM/UIM)
- Covers bodily injury in all states
- Covers property damage in some states
- Recommended to match your liability limits

### Q: Does uninsured motorist cover hit-and-run accidents?

**Covered:**
- You're hit by unknown driver who flees

- Passengers' injuries

**Requirements:**
1. **Police Report Required:**
   - Must file within 24 hours

3. **Reasonable Attempt to Identify Driver:**
   - Note plate number if possible
   - Description of vehicle
   - Direction of travel

## Scenario: I need an SR-22. What is it and how do I get one?

**What Is an SR-22?**

**Definition:**
- Certificate of Financial Responsibility
- Proof of insurance filed with state
- Required after serious violations
- Not a type of insurance, just a filing

**When Required:**
- DUI/DWI conviction
- Driving without insurance
- Multiple violations in short period
- At-fault accident without insurance
- License suspension/revocation
- Court order

**How It Works:**
- Insurance company files SR-22 with state
- Electronically transmitted
- Continuous proof of insurance
- If policy cancels, state is notified immediately
- License suspended if insurance lapses

**How to Get SR-22:**

**Step 1: Contact Insurance**
- Call: 1-800-EIP-CARE
- Request SR-22 filing
- Provide: Case/citation number, court info

**Step 2: Pay Filing Fee**
- One-time fee: $15-25
- Covers electronic filing with state
- State may charge separate fee

**Step 3: Filing Process**
- We file electronically with state
- Processed within 1-3 business days
- You receive confirmation
- State confirms receipt

**Step 4: Maintain Coverage**
- CANNOT let insurance lapse
- Lapse triggers license suspension
- Suspension notice sent to DMV immediately
- Must maintain for required period (3-5 years typically)

**Premium Impact:**
- Violation that caused SR-22 increases rates (not SR-22 itself)
- DUI: 80-300% increase
- Driving without insurance: 50-100% increase
- May need high-risk insurance

**Duration:**
- Typically 3-5 years (state dependent)
- Continuous coverage required
- Cannot cancel insurance during SR-22 period
- After period, state releases requirement

**Cost:**
- Filing fee: $15-25
- Rate increase: Depends on violation (may be hundreds per month)

**Important:**
- Never let coverage lapse
- Pay premiums on time
- Auto-pay strongly recommended
- Even one-day lapse restarts the clock
